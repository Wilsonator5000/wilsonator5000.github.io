DESIGN

It's really simple.


SuperHomework does it for you.

Gone are the days of announcements and agenda books, and the web is here to stay. Your teacher has probably already adopted this change, and posts their homework on a website or other electronic medium. But there's one current problem with this system: there is no organization, and you as a student end up wasting time navigating across multiple sites and sources while trying to do your homework.

SuperHomework can simplify all of this. It automatically pulls the nightly homework from your teachers' websites and puts it into an accessible format. Simply open the app to view — that's it! Or get notifications with a customized summary every afternoon without lifting a finger.

FOR TEACHERS

You don't have to do a thing.

If you have a blog, you're all set.

Seriously. If your homework blog is compatible with SuperHomework — and chances are it will be — then you don't have to configure anything. SuperHomework will automatically pull information from your site for students to recieve, all without any extra menus or submits on your part.


Compatibility requires that your school is available within SuperHomework and that your website is set up in a blog format with RSS enabled.

Contact

SuperHomework is not a full, guaranteed replacement for keeping track of your homework on your own. Not all teachers will post on their blogs daily, so posts may be inaccurate. It is your responsibility if you forget an assignment because SuperHomework did not remind you about it. SuperHomework is still in its early stages, so sometimes there may not be a post one day, among a number of other issues that may arise at any time. If you have any suggestions, feel free to send an email to the developer of SuperHomework (Wilson Gramer) at wgramer03@gmail.com.
